Bought Project vs Built Project — What Actually Happens at Interviews

There are two types of final year projects in India right now. One type gets you through college. The other type gets you hired. The difference matters more than most students realise — until they are sitting in an interview.

❌ The Bought Project
  • You paid Rs.2,000–10,000 for code and a report
  • You cannot explain the architecture under pressure
  • You cannot answer "what was the hardest bug you fixed?"
  • The interviewer has seen 50 identical projects this week
  • You get through college submission — and nothing else
  • Zero portfolio value — no GitHub, no demo, no story
✅ The Built Project
  • You built it on real hardware — you own every decision
  • You can explain the entire stack: sensor → MCU → AI → cloud
  • You have a GitHub repo with 12 weeks of commit history
  • You can put the device on the table and demo it live
  • You pass college submission AND the placement interview
  • It becomes a career story you use for 3+ years
💬
From hiring managers and R&D leads we work with consistently: The message is the same across Bosch, Tata Elxsi, EV startups, and IoT product companies in Bangalore — "Send us candidates who have built real-time projects and are hands-on with the tools — coding, hardware, and domain application." They are not looking for certificates. They are looking for engineers who have held a device they built and can explain every component in it. That candidate is rare. That candidate is hired fast.

One of our ECE students from VTU — final year, no prior AI experience — built a working gesture recognition device during our program. He demoed it at a placement interview at a Bangalore IoT startup and was hired within 6 weeks. The interviewer told him he was the only candidate who had brought something they could hold in their hands. That is not luck. That is what happens when you build instead of buy.
